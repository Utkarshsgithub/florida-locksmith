Drilling the safe
Drilling the safe may be faster than manipulating it, but it does result in slight cosmetic damage. The safe is drilled with a small hole. The type of safe and how the safe lock is attached determine where the hole is bored. A scoping tool is sent through the hole and through the hard plate and into the lock to allow the locksmith to examine how the safe unlocks. Drilling has become more difficult since security features make bypassing a safe's lock more difficult.
Scoping the safe
Scoping the safe is similar to drilling, only a borescope is put into the drilled hole to view the safe's interior components. This procedure is utilized when the locksmith has to get beyond additional security measures.
Safe manipulation
When it comes to unlocking a safe, locksmiths prefer to manipulate the safe. This is because this procedure will not harm your safe or its contents. While attempting to operate the safe, our locksmiths will carefully listen to it. They adjust the dial and its wheels until everything is in place. They know the unlocking process is beginning when they hear the sound created when the driver pins contact the notch on the flywheel.
